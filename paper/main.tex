\title{Protocol-Sensitive Evaluation of BNNeck and ArcFace Embeddings for Cross-Session Palmprint Recognition}
\author{PALM\_CGK\_BASE Project Team}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Cross-session palmprint recognition requires embeddings that remain discriminative under acquisition shift and reliable at strict false-acceptance operating points. This paper evaluates a ResNet18 embedding model with BNNeck and ArcFace supervision for palmprint recognition under both the original seen-identity Tongji protocol and a stricter development/test palm-class-disjoint Tongji protocol. In the original seen-identity Tongji diagnostic setting, BNNeck + ArcFace appears favorable relative to the current CE + SupCon baseline. However, under the stricter development/test palm-class-disjoint Tongji protocol, BNNeck + ArcFace does not improve over CE + SupCon overall: its bidirectional three-seed average trails the baseline by 1.18 percentage points in Rank-1 and has an EER that is 1.01 percentage points worse. An IITD palm-class-disjoint within-session protocol is included as secondary validation and shows a near-tie between BNNeck + ArcFace and the CE + SupCon baseline rather than a clear improvement. Additional strict ablations show that BNNeck + CE is the best-performing tested component combination on the main strict Tongji metrics, while ArcFace-based variants are not consistently beneficial. The supported contribution is therefore not a universal method-improvement claim, but a protocol-sensitive evaluation of BNNeck and ArcFace embeddings for palmprint recognition.
\end{abstract}
