\begin{abstract}
Cross-session palmprint recognition requires embeddings that remain discriminative under acquisition shift and reliable at strict false-acceptance operating points. This paper evaluates a ResNet18 embedding model with BNNeck and ArcFace supervision for palmprint recognition under both the original seen-identity Tongji protocol and a stricter development/test subject-disjoint Tongji protocol. In the original seen-identity Tongji setting, BNNeck + ArcFace improves over the current CE + SupCon baseline. However, under the stricter development/test subject-disjoint Tongji protocol, BNNeck + ArcFace does not improve over CE + SupCon overall: its bidirectional three-seed average trails the baseline by 1.18 percentage points in Rank-1 and has an EER that is 1.01 percentage points worse. An IITD within-dataset subject-disjoint validation provides a mixed near-tie result and is treated as secondary evidence only. The supported contribution is therefore not a universal method-improvement claim, but a protocol-sensitive evaluation of BNNeck and ArcFace embeddings for palmprint recognition.
\end{abstract}
