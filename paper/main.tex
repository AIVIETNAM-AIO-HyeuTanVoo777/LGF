\begin{abstract}
Cross-session palmprint recognition requires embeddings that remain discriminative under session shift and reliable at strict false-acceptance operating points. This paper reports the current evidence from the PALM\_CGK\_BASE project for a margin-aware ResNet18 embedding model with BNNeck and ArcFace supervision. Under bidirectional 3-seed Tongji evaluation, the proposed B6 model improves over a strong ResNet18 + CE + SupCon baseline by +1.70 percentage points in Rank-1, -0.51 percentage points in EER, and +3.63 percentage points in TAR@FAR=1e-3. The method also outperforms the fixed-Gabor B2 variant on the same multi-seed Tongji protocol while using fewer parameters and FLOPs than B2. On IITD within split, B6 remains competitive but does not universally outperform the CE + SupCon baseline. We therefore make a scoped claim: BNNeck + ArcFace improves cross-session Tongji palmprint verification over the current strong baseline, without claiming state-of-the-art or universal superiority.
\end{abstract}
