Under this stricter protocol, B6 does not improve over B1 overall. Relative to B1, B6 changes Rank-1 by -1.18 percentage points, Rank-5 by -1.00 percentage point, Macro-F1 by -1.31 percentage points, EER by +1.01 percentage points (worse), TAR@FAR=$10^{-2}$ by -2.39 percentage points, and TAR@FAR=$10^{-3}$ by -2.07 percentage points. The result therefore contradicts a universal-improvement claim for BNNeck + ArcFace under held-out subject evaluation.

The directional analysis shows that the failure is not symmetric. In S1$\rightarrow$S2, B6 gives a limited strict low-FAR gain of +1.19 percentage points in TAR@FAR=$10^{-3}$ but trails B1 on Rank-1, Rank-5, Macro-F1, EER, and TAR@FAR=$10^{-2}$. In S2$\rightarrow$S1, B6 underperforms B1 across all reported metrics, including -2.22 percentage points in Rank-1 and -5.33 percentage points in TAR@FAR=$10^{-3}$. This direction sensitivity is the central empirical finding of the stricter Tongji evaluation.

\subsection{Result Summary}

The combined evidence changes the paper framing. BNNeck + ArcFace improves the original seen-identity Tongji protocol, but it does not improve over CE + SupCon overall under the stricter development/test subject-disjoint Tongji protocol. IITD provides mixed secondary validation only. The supported contribution is therefore a protocol-sensitive evaluation of BNNeck and ArcFace embeddings for palmprint recognition, not a universal method-improvement claim.
