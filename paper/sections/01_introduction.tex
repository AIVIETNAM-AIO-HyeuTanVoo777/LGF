\section{Introduction}

Palmprint recognition is commonly evaluated as an identification and verification problem, and recent deep-learning surveys describe a broad shift from handcrafted texture descriptors toward learned feature representations \cite{gao2025deeplearning}. Cross-session recognition is a challenging setting because acquisition changes can alter illumination, pose, deformation, and sensor conditions between recording sessions. In the PALM\_CGK\_BASE project, the Tongji protocol separates session directions into S1$\rightarrow$S2 and S2$\rightarrow$S1, enabling a direct test of session shift.

The current strong baseline is B1, a ResNet18 model trained with cross entropy and supervised contrastive learning \cite{khosla2020supervised}. The evaluated variant is B6, which combines a ResNet18 backbone, a 256-D embedding projection, BNNeck \cite{luo2019strong}, and an ArcFace normalized classifier \cite{deng2019arcface}. During evaluation, the post-BN embedding is L2-normalized and compared with cosine similarity.

The central result is protocol-sensitive. BNNeck + ArcFace improves the original seen-identity Tongji protocol, but it does not improve over CE + SupCon overall under the stricter development/test subject-disjoint Tongji protocol. Under this stricter protocol, B6 trails B1 by 1.18 percentage points in bidirectional three-seed Rank-1 and has an EER that is 1.01 percentage points worse.

This paper therefore reframes the method evidence as protocol-sensitive. The test protocol matters: results that look favorable in a seen-identity setting do not necessarily transfer to held-out subjects at cross-session operating points. The IITD subject-disjoint within-dataset evaluation is used only as secondary validation, where the result is mixed and does not support a broad dominance claim.

The contributions of this paper are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A subject-disjoint cross-session evaluation of B1 CE + SupCon and B6 BNNeck + ArcFace on Tongji.
    \item A protocol-sensitivity analysis showing that BNNeck + ArcFace does not consistently improve under held-out subject evaluation.
    \item An IITD subject-disjoint within-dataset secondary validation showing a mixed/near-tie result.
    \item Reproducible splits, configs, and result summaries for the scoped evaluation.
\end{itemize}
