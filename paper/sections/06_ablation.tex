\section{Ablation Analysis}
\label{sec:ablation}

This section separates two kinds of ablation evidence. The primary ablation evidence uses the same strict Tongji palm-class-disjoint protocol as the main result table. The older seen-identity seed42 ablation is reported as diagnostic context because it explains why BNNeck and ArcFace initially appeared promising, but it is not used as primary evidence for the paper claim.

\subsection{Strict Palm-Class-Disjoint Ablation}

The strict ablation compares six ResNet18 variants under the same Tongji development/test palm-class-disjoint protocol, two session directions, and three random seeds. B0 uses cross entropy only. B1 adds supervised contrastive learning. B4 uses ArcFace without BNNeck. B5 uses BNNeck with cross entropy only. B6 combines BNNeck and ArcFace. B7 adds a light supervised contrastive term to BNNeck + ArcFace.

\input{sections/strict_tongji_ablation_table.tex}

Table~\ref{tab:strict_tongji_ablation} shows that the strongest strict-protocol ablation row is B5, not B6 or B7. B5 reaches the best bidirectional Rank-1 average and the best strict TAR@FAR=$10^{-3}$ average among the tested variants. Compared with B0, B5 improves Rank-1 by 0.85 percentage points. Compared with B1, B5 improves Rank-1 by 0.43 percentage points and TAR@FAR=$10^{-3}$ by 0.88 percentage points. These gains are modest, but they indicate that BNNeck with standard cross-entropy supervision is useful in this setting.

The ArcFace-based variants are less consistent. B4 does not improve over B0 overall, B6 trails B1 in Rank-1 and low-FAR verification, and B7 has high directional variance. In particular, B7 improves the S2$\rightarrow$S1 direction relative to B6 but is unstable in S1$\rightarrow$S2. This supports a component-level interpretation: BNNeck can be beneficial, but ArcFace and light SupCon do not provide a reliable improvement under the strict Tongji palm-class-disjoint protocol.

The seen-identity diagnostic shows why B6 was worth evaluating under a stricter protocol: ArcFace and BNNeck improved the original diagnostic metrics, and light SupCon in B7 did not materially change the result relative to B6. However, the strict palm-class-disjoint ablation reverses the interpretation. The primary evidence is therefore the strict protocol, under which the best-performing tested component combination is BNNeck + CE and the ArcFace-based variants are not consistently superior.
